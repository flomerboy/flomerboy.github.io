Unexpected interaction with a brick wall was a study in affordances with Annice Chen. We started our exploration by using a laser cutter to make seams without the use of thread. This enabled us to design garments that could be torn from a flat plane. We devised a co-creation experience, wherein the garment is provisional until the model completes its creation in an enabling and expressive gesture.

This work was a result of a deep exploration in the material properties of fusible interfacing and the manufacturing process of the laser cutter.
